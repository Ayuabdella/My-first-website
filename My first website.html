CHOCOLATE

Introductio

Chocolate or cocoa is a food (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_food) made from roasted and ground cocoa seed (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cocoa_bean) kernels that is available as a liquid, solid, or paste, either on its own or as a flavoring agent (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flavoring_agent) in other foods. The seeds of the cacao tree (Theobroma cacao (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theobroma_cacao)) have an intense bitter (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bitter_(taste)) taste and must be fermented (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermentation_(food)) to develop the flavor. After fermentation (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermentation), the seeds are dried, cleaned, and roasted.

Types

chocolate are used in baking and confectionery. These include baking chocolate (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baking_chocolate) (often unsweetened), couverture chocolate (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Couverture_chocolate) (used for coating), compound chocolate (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compound_chocolate) (a lower-cost alternative) and modeling chocolate (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modeling_chocolate). Modeling chocolate is a chocolate paste made by melting chocolate and combining it with corn syrup (https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corn_syrup), glucose syrup.
